The CPM framework: four categories, 13+ measures

The R&I Collaboration Progress Monitor provides a systematic, evidence-based view of university–business collaboration across the UK. Three established pillars cover engagement volume, financial investment and commercial outputs — and the fourth pillar, now live with its first metrics, uses UKRI's Gateway to Research (GtR) to capture publicly funded collaborative R&D at the project level.

Collaboration activity 01
2 measures

Counts the volume of formal interactions between UK universities and business, distinguishing SMEs (under 250 employees) from large businesses.

  1. Total interactions with SMEs
  2. Total interactions with large business
Investment in collaboration 02
8 measures

Tracks the financial flows that underpin collaborative R&D: income from business partnerships, industry contributions to HE research spend, and Innovate UK grant funding.

  1. Business collaboration income
  2. % of HE collaboration income from business
  3. Domestic industry funds in HE R&D
  4. Overseas industry funds in HE R&D
  5. Income from interactions with SMEs
  6. Income from interactions with large business
  7. Total Innovate UK grants
  8. Income per Innovate UK grant
Products of collaboration 03
3 measures

Captures tangible commercial outputs: licensed intellectual property, licensing income earned, and active spinout companies formed from HE research.

  1. Number of licences granted
  2. Total licensing income
  3. Academic spinouts
R&D collaboration (GtR) 04 LIVE
28 measures · 4 dimensions

Built from UKRI's Gateway to Research: every publicly funded R&I project with a business partner, linked to universities, funders, mechanisms and DSIT innovation-cluster geography.

  1. Volume & scale — 6 measures (links, partners, grant value)
  2. Intensity & productivity — 10 measures (per-institution rates, repeat pairs, KTP vs collaborative R&D)
  3. Innovation depth — 4 measures (IP links, spinouts, publications, further funding)
  4. Diversity & concentration — 8 measures (geography, DSIT clusters, funder concentration)

How the four pillars connect

Each pillar measures a different stage of the same relationship: activity creates the pipeline, investment fuels it, GtR captures the publicly co-funded core, and products are what comes out the other end.

Why add a GtR pillar?

HE-BCI captures income flowing into universities, but says little about the structure of collaboration. Gateway to Research lists every UKRI-funded project together with its participating organisations, so it can show who collaborates with whom, on what, and how partnership networks evolve. It also covers collaboration that generates no direct HE income — in-kind partnerships, consortium membership and co-created research agendas — that the current monitor misses. Linking GtR organisation identifiers to Companies House would additionally allow the monitor to profile the size, sector and geography of partner firms.

Reading this page

The CAGR shows the average yearly rate at which a measure has increased or decreased over five years. Significant means 10%+ a year in either direction; moderate is 3–10%; stable is within ±3%. Because income is lumpy and contract-driven, smaller providers swing between bands more readily — compare with the funnel plot on the Overview sub-tab. Switch metrics above to see how growth patterns differ between income, engagement volumes and commercialisation.
